Saving the Honey Bees!

A Little About The Honey Bee

The Honey bee is an extrordinary insect that lives in many climates and areas on our planet ranging from Europe all the way to the Arctic Circle. They live in large colonies with many of their kind in nests made of wax called hives. Honeybees are great pollinators, or a species who moves the pollen of one plant to another. This process is very important to many life forms on the planet. Cross pollination from bees benefits at least 30% of all crops. Without bees to spread seeds, and pollinate many plants, including food crops will die off.

Foods That Will Be Impacted

  • A majority of almost all fruits, and vegitables.
  • Sugarcane, agave, honey, and vanilla.
  • Beans, and nuts (coffee, and chocolate!)

Things You Can Do

Since honey bees are so important to so many species on earth, you may be wondering what you can do at home to help these amazing insects not go extinct.

  • Stop using pestacides on your lawns. (especially weed killing products!)
  • Plant bee friendly flowers in your garden.
  • Buy local raw honey.
  • Try to buy local organic food.
  • Learn more about beekeeping, and sustainable practices.
  • Share your knowlege with your community!
